Audit result: Stage A was already implemented by M2. The CPU computes `camera_radius_meters` and `camera_radius_squared` from f64 altitude, then casts for upload. WGSL uses `sqrt(cam_radius_sq + 2*t*(C.D) + t*t)` for radius and matching quadratic shell intersections; it does not use `length(C + tD)` for the cancellation-sensitive radius test.

Added two deterministic regressions:
- shader-equivalent f32 radius evaluation across 0.1m, 1m, 10m, 1.7km, and 10km altitudes plus radial-to-grazing rays; maximum permitted surface-radius error is 0.5m;
- proof that camera radius is squared in f64 before the f32 upload cast.

All 111 app tests pass. Quadro `descent_to_10m` ray captures completed at 6,000km, 1,000km, 200km, 20km, 2km, 200m, and 10m; metrics stayed finite and no radial/depth bands appeared. The scenario manifest only fails raster-specific L18/resident-chunk assertions because ray mode intentionally has zero raster chunks.

Stage B's hi/lo camera-position split was not added: the guide makes it conditional on observed deck banding, and neither the numerical bound nor the 10m capture triggered it. The visibly coarse near-surface field is fixed-L4 data resolution, not precision loss.
